This blog post, written by Dr. Howard Schubiner, offers a fresh perspective on severe migraines, a condition that affects millions of Americans. For many people, migraines are a debilitating experience that disrupts daily life, and despite trying various treatments, they find little to no relief. The traditional view of migraines as a genetic disorder can be limiting, as it may lead to a focus on managing symptoms rather than addressing the underlying causes. Dr. Schubiner's post presents an alternative approach, one that explores the role of the mind in perpetuating migraine pain. By sharing the story of a patient who found cure through reappraising her life, this post highlights the potential for people to take an active role in their healing journey.

- Migraines are often viewed as a genetic disorder, but this perspective may not tell the whole story, and may even limit treatment options
- A significant portion of people with migraines, about 10%, experience frequent and severe headaches that don't respond to conventional treatments
- The patient featured in the post found relief from her migraines by reappraising her life, suggesting that the mind plays a crucial role in perpetuating pain
- This approach emphasizes the importance of addressing emotional and psychological factors, rather than just relying on medication or other traditional treatments

For someone living with chronic pain, including migraines, this post offers a message of hope and empowerment. By recognizing that the mind has a significant impact on our experience of pain, we can begin to explore new avenues for healing. This might involve examining our thoughts, emotions, and behaviors, and making conscious changes to reduce stress and promote well-being. By taking a more holistic approach to our health, we can potentially break free from the cycle of chronic pain and find a more fulfilling life.
